- **Sentiment accuracy only moved 4 points (52% → 56%).** The prompt-level guidance on directional calls did not substantially correct the model's underlying bias.
- **The two failure modes from the baseline persist:** (1) over-neutralizing on clear negative events, (2) over-directional on preview-style articles. One regression introduced: JPM S&P target reset flipped from neutral (correct coincidentally) to bearish.

**Interpretation:**
The failure modes are primarily about **financial-domain calibration**, not prompt wording. Prompt engineering can teach scoping decisions ("is this article about X?") but cannot inject domain knowledge ("which kinds of events move stocks and in which direction?"). Further prompt iteration is likely to yield diminishing returns.

**Proposed next interventions (in priority order):**
1. **Few-shot examples in the prompt** — include 3-5 canonical labeled examples (one bullish, one bearish, one neutral preview, one low-relevance) so the model has in-context calibration anchors.
2. **RAG over historical news + stock reactions** — retrieve analogous past events and their market outcomes to ground directional calls in precedent rather than abstract reasoning.
3. **LLM-as-judge layer** — run a second, stronger model over uncertain classifications to sanity-check them.
